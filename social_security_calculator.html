What Affects Your Benefit
Full Retirement Age (FRA) is based on your birth year. Claiming before FRA permanently reduces your benefit — up to 30% if you were born after 1959 and claim at 62. Waiting beyond FRA earns delayed retirement credits of 8% per year (2/3% per month), up to age 70.

COLA adjustments apply annually to all recipients regardless of when they claimed. Benefits are based on your highest 35 years of indexed earnings (AIME) and applied to the Primary Insurance Amount (PIA) formula.

Breakeven Age Summary
How to interpret: The breakeven age is when your cumulative lifetime benefits from waiting surpass what you'd have collected by claiming early. If you live past the breakeven age, delaying was financially beneficial. If you don't, claiming early paid off more. Health, need for income, and tax implications also matter.
