Planning Strategies Overview
Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT): Life insurance held in an ILIT passes outside the estate. The death benefit is not included in the taxable estate, potentially saving 40% in federal estate taxes.

Qualified Personal Residence Trust (QPRT): Transfers your home to heirs at a discounted gift tax value, removing future appreciation from the estate.

Grantor Retained Annuity Trust (GRAT): Transfer assets to a trust while retaining an annuity. If assets appreciate faster than the IRS hurdle rate (7520 rate), the excess passes to heirs estate-tax-free.

Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT): Receive income from a trust during your lifetime, with the remainder going to charity. Provides income, a current charitable deduction, and estate reduction.

Family Limited Partnership (FLP): Move assets into an FLP and transfer limited partnership interests to heirs, potentially at a valuation discount of 15–40% for lack of control and marketability.
